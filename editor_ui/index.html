About compozeFlow

compozeFlow is a powerful open-source video batch processing framework designed to streamline the creation and editing of video content. Whether you're a content creator, marketer, or video enthusiast, compozeFlow provides the tools you need to efficiently produce high-quality videos.

With compozeFlow, you can:

  • Create video assemblies from templates
  • Process multiple video files in batches
  • Apply consistent styling and branding across videos
  • Automate repetitive video editing tasks
  • Export videos in various formats and resolutions

Tips & Tricks

Organize Your Content Sources

Keep your video files organized in dedicated folders to make them easier to find and use in your video assemblies.

Use Templates for Consistency

Create and save templates for recurring video types to maintain a consistent style and save time on future projects.

Keyboard Shortcuts

Learn the keyboard shortcuts to speed up your workflow. Press Ctrl+N (or Cmd+N on Mac) for a new video assembly and Ctrl+O (or Cmd+O on Mac) to open an existing one.

Frequently Asked Questions

What file formats does compozeFlow support?
compozeFlow supports most common video formats including MP4, AVI, MOV, MKV, and more. It also supports various audio formats (MP3, WAV, AAC) and image formats (JPG, PNG, GIF).
Can I create my own templates?
Yes! You can create custom templates by saving any video assembly as a template. This allows you to reuse your preferred structure and settings for future projects.
How do I add content sources to my project?
You can add content sources by clicking on the "Add Content Source" button in the Content Sources panel and selecting a folder containing your media files.
Where are my video assemblies saved?
By default, video assemblies are saved in the "video_assemblies" folder in the application directory, but you can choose to save them anywhere on your computer.
